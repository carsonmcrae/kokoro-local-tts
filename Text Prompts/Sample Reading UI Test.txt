He didn’t realize anything was wrong at first.

That was the problem.

Everything looked normal. The same hallway. The same scuffed baseboards. The same faint hum of the lights overhead. If anything, it felt quieter than usual, like the building was holding its breath.

He took another step.

Something shifted.

Not in front of him. Not behind him. Just… off. Like a memory trying to line itself up and missing by an inch.

He stopped.

“I’ve been here before,” he said, mostly to himself.

The words didn’t sound right once they left his mouth.

Because he hadn’t.

He knew he hadn’t.

And yet, there was a certainty settling in his chest, slow and heavy, like it had been waiting for him to notice.

He looked down the hallway again.

This time, he wasn’t sure what he was expecting to see.

But he knew, with a strange and uncomfortable clarity, that whatever happened next…

wasn’t going to feel new.
